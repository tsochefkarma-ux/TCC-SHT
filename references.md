# References & Data Sources

The Computational Cosmos framework is built upon the following peer-reviewed mathematical and physical results.

## 1. Geometry & Topology
* **Hosono, S., Saito, M. H., & Stienstra, J. (1997).** "On the Mirror Symmetry Conjecture for Schoen's Calabi-Yau 3-folds." *arXiv:alg-geom/9709027*.
    * *Source of:* Intersection Tensor ($\kappa_{ijk}$), Hodge Numbers ($h^{1,1}=3$), and $E_8$ lattice connection.
* **Constantin, A., He, Y. H., & Lukas, A. (2024).** "Counting String Vacua and the Standard Model."
    * *Source of:* Bundle cohomology techniques and generation counting ($N_{gen} = |\chi|/2$).

## 2. Computational Physics
* **Lloyd, S. (2002).** "Computational Capacity of the Universe." *Physical Review Letters*.
    * *Source of:* The $10^{120}$ operations limit and the information bound ($10^{90}$ bits).
* **Bousso, R. (2002).** "The Holographic Principle." *Reviews of Modern Physics*.
    * *Source of:* The area law entropy bound used in the "Planck Floor" derivation.

## 3. Heterotic Phenomenology
* **Donagi, R., & Ovrut, B. A. (2004).** "Standard Model Bundles."
    * *Source of:* Vector bundle construction for $SU(3) \times SU(2) \times U(1)$.
* **Arkani-Hamed, N., & Dimopoulos, S. (2005).** "Supersymmetric Unification without Low Energy Supersymmetry."
    * *Source of:* The "Split SUSY" phenomenological constraints matched by our 280 TeV prediction.

## 4. Experimental Data
* **Planck Collaboration (2018).** "Cosmological Parameters."
    * *Source of:* $H_0$, $\Omega_\Lambda$, $\Omega_m$, $n_s$.
* **Particle Data Group (2024).** "Review of Particle Physics."
    * *Source of:* Quark/Lepton masses, couplings ($\alpha$), and exclusion limits.
